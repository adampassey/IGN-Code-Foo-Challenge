My Fire

For as long as I can remember, my greatest passion has been video games.

As a kid, I remember running home from the school bus. If I beat my older brother home, I'd get to play games first. That excitement has stuck with me my whole life. In fact, a little over a week ago I woke up before the sun rose to pick up L.A. Noire as early as possible on launch day.

I've always been easily immersed in video games which is why I started teaching myself C++ in my early teen years with the intent of developing video games as a profession. I wanted to share all the excitement and the passion I have for video games with other people. I wanted them to experience games in the same way I had all my life.

After finishing high school, I studied computer science at Southern Oregon University. Unfortunately, C++ was not in their curriculum. Unhappy with only learning Java, I took the opportunity to begin the web development department at my family business: Passey Advertising, Inc. My father had started and ran the company for over 20 years by the time I came on board.

It didn't take long for my programming skills to translate to the web- and even less time for the department to flourish. Before I knew it, I was leading a small team of passionate developers. Also, I was getting the chance to consult and write applications for well-respected corporations, start-ups, and mom & pop shops.

Despite the success, I've always wanted to work in the video game industry. I'm a longtime viewer of IGN. I appreciate and respect what IGN does for the gaming industry. I want nothing more than to work at such an awesome place with such passionate people in an industry that I love.

Hopefully everything I have put together for this challenge shows the passion I have- for the web, video games, and IGN.

Give me a chance. You won't be disappointed.
